This dashboard summarizes employee demographics, attrition trends, and job satisfaction across departments, education fields, and age bands.

Attrition Trends

  • By Department: Highest attrition in R&D (133) and Sales (92); HR is comparatively low (12).
  • By Education Field: Turnover is highest in Life Sciences (89) and Medical (63).
  • By Age Group & Gender: Attrition concentrates among 25–34 (especially males), followed by 35–44.

Employee Demographics

Male headcount is higher across most age groups. The youngest (under 25) and oldest (over 55) cohorts contribute less to attrition than mid-career employees.

Job Satisfaction

Job satisfaction varies by role. Sales Executives and Research Scientists show relatively stronger satisfaction levels, while Human Resources and some director roles trend lower—areas for closer review.

Strategic Insights & Actions

  • Retain Mid-Career Talent: Target the 25–34 cohort with growth paths, mentorship, and work-life initiatives.
  • Department Focus: Investigate root causes in R&D and Sales (leadership, workload, compensation, role clarity).
  • Education-Specific Plans: Tailor development and progression frameworks for Life Sciences and Medical backgrounds.
  • Elevate Satisfaction: Address pain points in HR and director-track roles to reduce risk of further turnover.
