Road Accidents Dashboard Summary

The Road Accidents Dashboard provides a comprehensive overview of accident data, highlighting key metrics such as casualties, accident causes, and road conditions. This enables stakeholders to identify high-risk areas and implement effective safety measures.

Key Insights

  • Total Casualties: 417,883 recorded, including:
    • 7,135 Fatal Casualties
    • 59,312 Serious Casualties
    • 351,436 Slight Casualties
  • Vehicle Types: Cars accounted for the majority of casualties (333,485), followed by motorcycles (33,672) and vans (33,472).
  • Monthly Trends: Accident numbers were relatively consistent across months, with slight increases observed toward year-end.
  • Road Type: Most casualties occurred on single carriageways (309.7K), followed by dual carriageways (67.4K).
  • Road Surface: Majority of accidents occurred on dry roads, with fewer cases on wet or snow/ice surfaces.
  • Location: Urban areas had higher casualties (255,864) compared to rural areas (162,019).
  • Light Conditions: Most accidents happened during the day (304,963) compared to night (112,920).

Conclusion

The dashboard reveals that cars are the most involved in accidents, with urban areas and daytime conditions contributing to higher casualty counts. Single carriageways remain the most dangerous road type. These insights can help policymakers, law enforcement, and transport agencies develop targeted strategies to improve road safety and reduce casualties.
